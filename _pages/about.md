I am a soon-to-be graduate from McGill University in Montreal, Canada. My degree is in political science and economics, which has involved plenty of reading, writing, and analysis, and I’ve complemented this with work in applied data analysis and quantitative methods across coursework, research assistantships, and internships in the charity sector. 

I am interested in using good data to make smart decisions, whether it’s policy research, product analysis, or market research, and am actively seeking roles in these fields. On this page, you can find examples of my [research work](/research/) in quantitative social science, including work done under Professors [Leonardo Baccini](https://sites.google.com/site/leonardobaccini/) and [Elissa Berwick](https://www.elissaberwick.com/). You can also find [my CV](/cv/) and [compilations of things I like](/reading/), such as books, articles, and blogs. 

Outside of school, I serve as the Editor-in-Chief of [The McGill International Review](https://www.mironline.ca/), which publishes articles on a range of international affairs. I like knitting and documenting my projects with extensive notes (for those inclined, I can be found on [Ravelry](https://www.ravelry.com/people/stellarzhang)). I love cooking, but I loathe baking; I play pickleball well, tennis less so; and I write everything with a fountain pen (my Lamy AL-Star, to be precise).  

Thank you for visiting my site. I can be contacted at [stellar.zhang@mail.mcgill.ca](mailto:stellar.zhang@mail.mcgill.ca).
